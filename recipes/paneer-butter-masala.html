Paneer Butter Masala

Description

Paneer Butter Masala is a rich, creamy, and mildly spiced North Indian dish made with paneer (Indian cottage cheese), butter, tomatoes, cream, and aromatic spices. This restaurant-style dish pairs perfectly with naan, roti, or jeera rice, making it a favorite among vegetarians.

Ingredients

For the Gravy:

  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 3 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 10-12 cashews (soaked in warm water)
  • 1 green chili (optional)
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • ½ cup fresh cream
  • 1 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • Salt to taste

For the Paneer:

  • 200g paneer (cubed)
  • 1 tbsp butter (for sautéing, optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Cashew Paste:

    1. Blend the soaked cahsews with a little water to make a smooth paste. Set aside.

  2. Cook the Gravy:

    1. Heat butter and oil in a pan. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
    2. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
    3. Pour in the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates.

  3. Add Spices:

    1. Stir in turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
    2. Add the cashew paste and mix well. Cook for another 2 minutes.

  4. Blend for a Smooth Gravy (Optional):

    1. Allow the mixture to cool slightly, then blend into a smooth paste. Strain for an extra creamy texture.

  5. Finish the Gravy:

    1. Return the blended mixture to the pan, add water, and bring it to a gentle simmer.
    2. Stir in kasuri methi, garam masala, and fresh cream. Mix well and cook for a minute.

  6. Prepare the Paneer:

    1. Optionally, sauté paneer cubes in butter for a slight golden crust.
    2. Add the paneer to the gravy and mix gently.

  7. Final Touch & Serve:

    1. Simmer for 2-3 minutes, then turn off the heat.
    2. Garnish with a drizzle of cream and fresh coriander leaves.
    3. Serve hot with naan, roti, or jeera rice.
